Photosynthesis and Cellular Respiration

Overview of Processes

Photosynthesis Mechanics

In chloroplasts, chlorophyll absorbs light for the light-dependent reactions in thylakoid membranes, producing ATP and NADPH via an electron transport chain, while splitting water releases oxygen. The Calvin cycle in the stroma uses these products to fix carbon dioxide into glucose, fueling plant growth and storage, a process refined in 2025 research.

Cellular Respiration Stages

Cellular respiration, occurring in mitochondria, includes glycolysis in the cytoplasm (breaking glucose into pyruvate), the Krebs cycle in the matrix (generating NADH and FADH2), and oxidative phosphorylation in the inner membrane, where electron transport drives ATP synthesis via chemiosmosis, with oxygen as the final electron acceptor, forming water.

Interconnectedness and Global Impact

Photosynthesis produces oxygen and glucose, which cellular respiration uses to generate ATP, releasing carbon dioxide for photosynthesis, forming a balanced cycle crucial to the global carbon cycle. This interplay, regulating atmospheric gases, supports climate stability, a key ecological focus in 2025 studies.

Practical Applications

Understanding these processes enhances agriculture by optimizing crop photosynthesis, informs bioenergy through sustainable fuel research, and aids medicine by addressing metabolic disorders like cancer.
