Hyena

What Are Hyenas?

Hyenas, carnivorous mammals of the Hyaenidae family, include four species: spotted, brown, striped, and aardwolf. Known for their vocalizations and scavenging, they thrive across Africa and parts of Asia, with spotted hyenas dominating sub-Saharan ecosystems as of May 2025.

Physical Characteristics

Spotted hyenas, the largest, weigh up to 160 pounds with powerful jaws and spotted coats. Brown hyenas (up to 90 pounds) adapt to arid regions, while striped (up to 90 pounds) and aardwolves (up to 30 pounds) feature unique stripes and termite-specialized tongues, showcasing diverse adaptations.

Social Structure and Behavior

Spotted hyenas form matriarchal clans with females dominant, using complex vocalizations for coordination. Brown and striped hyenas are solitary scavengers, while aardwolves target termites. In 2025, studies highlight their social bonds aiding survival in fragmented habitats.

Conservation Challenges

Facing habitat loss, hunting for meat, and cultural stigma, hyenas are threatened. Conservation efforts in May 2025 include habitat restoration, anti-poaching, and education to counter misconceptions, ensuring their ecological and cultural significance endures.
