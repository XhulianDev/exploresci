Stars and Stellar Evolution

Composition of Stars

Stars, primarily composed of hydrogen and helium, form from Big Bang elements. Through nuclear fusion, they create heavier elements, dispersing them into space upon death, shaping the universe’s chemical evolution as of May 2025.

Stellar Life Cycle

Stars form in nebulae, evolving from protostars to main sequence via fusion. Their lifespan depends on mass—massive stars last millions of years, while sun-like stars endure for 10 billion, transitioning into red giants.

End Stages of Stars

Low-mass stars become white dwarfs, cooling over billions of years. Massive stars explode as supernovae, forming neutron stars or black holes, with 2025 studies confirming their role in heavy element creation.

Galactic Evolution

Galaxies form hierarchically, merging smaller structures into massive systems. The cosmic web—clusters, filaments, and voids—reveals matter distribution, while supermassive black holes at galactic centers drive evolution, per 2025 data.

Search for Life

Studying habitable zones in galaxies assesses life’s potential. Conditions for liquid water and elements like carbon guide the search—2025 research targets these zones to estimate extraterrestrial life likelihood.
